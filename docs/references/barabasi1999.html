Summary

Introduces the Barabási-Albert model of scale-free networks with preferential attachment. Shows that real-world networks have power-law degree distributions and small diameters (d ~ ln n / ln ln n). This small-world property is central to STORM: since diameter governs incremental APSP performance, the small diameters of scale-free networks make STORM optimal for virtually all practical scenarios.
